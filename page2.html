Types of Personal Training

  • One-on-One Training
  • Group Training
  • Online Training
  • Specialized Training

Each type of training has its own benefits and one style is not a cookie cutter solution. If you want to get some friends and be social, group training might be your thing! If you are crunched for time, online training might be the best fit.

NASM OPT Model

The NASM OPT (Optimum Performance Training) Model is a training system that focuses on important phases for everyone. It consists of five phases:

  1. Stabilization Endurance
  2. Strength Endurance
  3. Hypertrophy
  4. Maximal Strength
  5. Power

The NASM OPT Model is designed to be flexible and adaptable to meet the needs of individuals with different fitness levels and goals. A personal trainer will assess your current fitness level and design a program that incorporates the appropriate phases of the OPT Model to help you achieve your specific goals.

Myths About Training

  • Myth 1: Personal training is only for athletes.
  • Myth 2: Personal trainers will make you do exercises you hate.
  • Myth 3: You need to be in shape before you start personal training.
  • Myth 4: Personal training is too expensive.
  • Myth 5: All personal trainers are the same.
